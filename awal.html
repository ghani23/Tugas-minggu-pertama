Fact about Muhammad Al-Fatih

  1. Mehmed continued his conquests in Anatolia with its reunification and in Southeast Europe as far west as Bosnia.
  2. He is considered a hero in modern-day Turkey and parts of the wider Muslim world.
  3. Mehmed II was born on March 30, 1432, in Edirne, then the capital city of the Ottoman state.
  4. The influence of Akshamsaddin in Mehmed’s life became predominant from a young age, especially in the imperative of fulfilling his Islamic duty to overthrow the Byzantine empire by conquering Constantinople.
  5. Around the grand mosque that he constructed, he erected eight madrasas, which, for nearly a century, kept their rank as the highest teaching institutions of the Islamic sciences in the empire.
  6. Mehmed II allowed his subjects a considerable degree of religious freedom, provided they were obedient to his rule.
  7. Within Constantinople, Mehmed established a millet, or an autonomous religious community, and appointed the former Patriarch Gennadius Scholarius as a religious leader for the Orthodox Christians of the city.
  8. Mehmed was the first sultan who was able to codify and implement kanunname solely based on his own independent authority.
